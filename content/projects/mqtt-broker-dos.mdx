## Problem

MQTT is the messaging backbone of much of the IoT world, but brokers are often
deployed with little thought to adversarial load. I wanted to measure exactly how
a realistic broker holds up under attack — and turn that into a repeatable
runbook.

## Approach

I deployed a full Mosquitto MQTT broker environment on a Raspberry Pi 4 with
authentication, ACLs, rate limiting, and a defined client topology to establish a
controlled baseline. Then I executed three attack vectors with custom Python
scripts:

- **Retained-message flood** — abusing retained messages to exhaust memory.
- **Wildcard topic storm** — forcing expensive subscription matching across the
  topic tree.
- **Session exhaustion** — opening and abandoning sessions to consume broker
  state.

## Outcome

Under load, the broker's memory climbed from roughly 600 MB to 8 GB and traffic
went from under 1 Mbit/s to 600–700 Mbit/s, producing a complete broker outage
lasting about 49 minutes. I isolated the root cause behind each vector, applied
targeted mitigations, and documented the full attack-to-remediation cycle as a
reproducible runbook — the kind of artifact a blue team can actually act on.
