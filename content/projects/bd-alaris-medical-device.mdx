## Problem

Clinical devices live for years on hospital networks, often unpatched. The BD
Alaris 8015 PC Unit infusion pump is exactly the kind of deployed, long-lived
device whose full attack surface deserves a careful look.

## Approach

I investigated `CVE-2016-9355` (sensitive data exposure) alongside a separately
identified `CWE-798` (use of hardcoded credentials) weakness in the BD Alaris
8015 PC Unit, working to map the device's complete attack surface rather than a
single bug in isolation. From there I built out the supporting analysis: a threat
model, a Data Flow Diagram (DFD) of how data and trust move through the device,
and credential XML samples illustrating the finding.

## Outcome

The result was a complete research deliverable package — term paper, threat
model, DFD, credential samples, and a presentation deck — that translated raw
vulnerability data into structured findings with concrete remediation
recommendations. It's a template for how to reason about the security of any
deployed embedded device, not just a single pump.
